# Speaker Notes
## AI as Your Professional Reflection Coach

**Presenter:** Ammar Javeed

**Duration:** Approximately 30 Minutes

---

# Opening (2 minutes)

### Objective

Introduce yourself and the workshop.

### What to Say

Good morning/afternoon everyone.

Today I'll be presenting my LiaScript workshop titled **"AI as Your Professional Reflection Coach."**

This workshop is designed around **one learning objective** from the UNESCO AI Competency Framework.

Instead of covering many AI topics, I focused on one specific competency: helping teachers use AI to support professional self-reflection through effective prompt engineering.

During the workshop, participants will complete interactive activities, quizzes, a case study, and a final practical task.

---

# Workshop Information

### Explain

Briefly explain:

- Duration
- Target audience
- Beginner level
- Interactive workshop

Do not spend more than one minute here.

# Why This Workshop?

### Explain

Teachers often reflect after teaching, but reflection is sometimes difficult without feedback.

AI can help teachers think about their lessons, suggest improvements, and ask reflective questions.

However...

AI only gives useful feedback if we know how to communicate with it effectively.

That is why prompt engineering is the main focus of this workshop.

# Icebreaker

Ask participants to think about:

"How do you usually reflect after teaching?"

Pause for around 20 seconds.

You don't need answers.

This simply activates prior knowledge.

---

# Sarah's Story

Explain Sarah's situation.

Mention that Sarah represents a realistic teaching scenario.

The purpose is to keep one continuous example throughout the workshop instead of introducing different examples every time.

---

# Activity 1

Prompt A vs Prompt B

Ask participants to read both prompts.

Before revealing the answer ask:

"Which prompt would produce more useful AI feedback?"

Allow participants time to think.

Reveal the correct answer.

Explain that Prompt B includes:

- context
- learner information
- teaching challenge
- clear request

---

# Why Prompt B Works

Explain the importance of context.

Mention that AI does not know what happened unless we explain it.

Better prompts generally produce better responses.

---

# Video 1

Introduce the video.

Tell participants:

"As you watch, think about what makes an AI prompt effective."

Play the video.

Afterwards ask:

"What was one idea from the video that you found useful?"

---

# Activity 2

Participants improve Sarah's weak prompt.

Walk around if possible.

Give approximately one minute.

Afterwards compare with the expert prompt.

Explain there is no perfect prompt.

The goal is continuous improvement.

---

# Video 2

Introduce the second video.

Explain that participants will immediately apply what they learn.

Play the video.

---

# Activity 3

Participants now use ChatGPT or imagine using it.

Explain that the activity focuses on reflection rather than getting perfect answers.

Encourage them to think critically about AI suggestions.

---

# Activity 4

Discuss the AI response.

Ask:

"Would you follow every AI suggestion?"

Allow discussion.

Explain that teachers remain responsible for professional decisions.

---

# Professional Development

Return to Sarah.

Explain how Sarah improved her next lesson.

Emphasize:

AI supported Sarah,

Sarah made the decisions.

This is responsible AI use.

---

# Knowledge Check

Quickly complete the quiz.

Explain why AI should never replace professional judgement.

---

# Responsible AI

Mention three ideas:

Protect privacy.

Verify information.

Use professional judgement.

Keep this section brief.

---

# Final Challenge

Explain that this is the main assessment.

Participants now demonstrate what they have learned.

Encourage them to include:

- lesson topic
- learners
- challenge
- feedback request

---

# Self Assessment

Explain that learners check their own prompts before using AI.

This helps them become independent learners.

---

# Final Assessment

Review Prompt A and Prompt B.

Ask participants why Prompt B is stronger.

Reveal the answer.

---

# Reflection

Give participants one minute.

Ask them to think about:

"What is one thing you will change when using AI after today's workshop?"

No right or wrong answer.

---

# Workshop Summary

Summarize three messages.

Reflection improves teaching.

Better prompts produce better AI feedback.

Teachers remain responsible for educational decisions.
