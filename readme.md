# AI as Your Professional Reflection Coach
## Designing Effective AI Prompts for Teacher Professional Development

![](media/cover.jpg)

---

## Workshop Information

**Duration:** 30 Minutes

**Target Audience:** TVET Teachers, Trainers, and Pre-service Teachers

**Level:** Beginner

**Delivery Mode:** Interactive Online Workshop

---

## Why This Workshop?

Teachers reflect on their lessons every day, but meaningful reflection is not always easy.

Artificial Intelligence (AI) can support teachers by providing ideas, asking reflective questions, and suggesting improvements. However, AI only provides useful support when teachers know how to write effective prompts and critically evaluate AI-generated responses.

This workshop helps participants learn how to use AI responsibly as a professional reflection partner while keeping teachers in control of all educational decisions.

---

This workshop is aligned with **Learning Objective LO5-3.2**, which focuses on combining AI tools with human coaching to support reflective practice, goal setting, and continuous professional development.

This workshop is designed around **one learning objective** from the UNESCO AI Competency Framework for Teachers.

### Learning Objective

> **Design and refine an effective AI prompt that supports professional self-reflection and contributes to continuous professional development.**

Every activity in this workshop helps participants achieve this single learning objective.

---

## Learning Success Criteria

By the end of this workshop, participants will be able to:

✅ Write an effective AI reflection prompt.

✅ Improve a weak prompt using prompt engineering techniques.

✅ Evaluate AI-generated feedback critically.

✅ Decide which AI suggestions are useful for professional practice.

✅ Create a final AI prompt for their own teaching context.

# Meet Sarah

![](media/teacher_reflection.jpg)

Sarah is a TVET teacher.

Today she taught a practical lesson on workplace safety.

After the lesson she noticed:

- Some students were confused.
- Others finished too early.
- One group did not participate.

Sarah wants to improve her teaching, but she is not sure what she should change.

She decides to ask an AI assistant for feedback.

But before she does that...

She needs to write a good prompt.

---

# Activity 1

## Which prompt would you choose?

Sarah writes two prompts.

### Prompt A

> My lesson was not good.
> Can you help?

### Prompt B

> I taught a 45-minute workplace safety lesson to first-year TVET students. During the practical activity, several students became confused about the safety procedures, while others completed the task very quickly. Act as an experienced instructional coach. Identify possible reasons why this happened and suggest three practical improvements I can apply in my next lesson.

Which prompt would produce more useful AI feedback?

[( )] Prompt A

[(X)] Prompt B

***

Prompt B gives the AI context, audience, lesson details, and a clear task. Better prompts produce better responses.

---

# Why Prompt B Works

Prompt B includes:

- Context
- Learner information
- Teaching challenge
- Clear task
- Specific request

These elements help AI generate more useful professional feedback.

Remember:

**Good AI begins with a good prompt.**

# Activity 2 – Improve the Prompt

Now it is your turn.

Read Sarah's first prompt.

> "My lesson was not good. Can you help?"

How could you improve it?

Think about:

- What lesson was taught?
- Who were the learners?
- What problem occurred?
- What type of feedback is needed?

Take one minute before continuing.

---

# An Improved Prompt

One possible answer is:

> Act as an experienced instructional coach.

> I taught a 45-minute practical lesson on workplace safety to first-year TVET students.

> During the lesson some students became confused during the practical activity while others finished much earlier than expected.

> Please identify possible reasons for these problems and suggest three practical improvements I can use in my next lesson.

Notice how the prompt provides:

- Context
- Teaching challenge
- Learner information
- Clear instructions

# Activity 3 – Try AI Yourself

# Activity 4 – Evaluate the AI Response

[( )] More demonstrations

[(X)] AI supports teacher reflection, but teachers make the final decisions.

---# Responsible Use of AI

![](media/ethics.jpg)

Artificial Intelligence is a valuable professional tool, but it should always be used responsibly.

Teachers remain responsible for:

- Making final educational decisions.
- Protecting student privacy.
- Checking AI-generated information for accuracy.
- Using professional judgement before applying AI suggestions.

AI should support teachers—not replace them.

| Question | Yes | No |

If you answered **Yes** to all four questions, your prompt is ready to test.
